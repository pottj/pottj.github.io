Welcome to my homepage! I am a genetic epidemiologist working on genome-wide association studies and causal inference models (aka Mendelian Randomization).

Research Objective

Statistical Genetics with focus on Women's Health

The goal of my research is to contribute to precision medicine through genetic epidemiology and to improve human health and well-being, especially regarding womenâ€™s health. For me, this includes analysing the molecular differences in multiple OMICS levels related to disease risk. The differences are either between the sexes or between age groups, e.g., before and after menopause. For this, I use genetic summary statistics from large-scale studies, genetic consortia, and electronic health data in causal models of Mendelian Randomization (MR).

Current projects

  • Lp(a) in pregnancy
  • MVMR of age at menarche and age at menopause on disease risk in women
  • Multivariate MR (MVMR) using longitudinal exposure data
  • Genetics and (MV)MR analyses of steroid hormones
  • Genetics and (MV)MR analyses of PCSK9 levels
