Welcome to my homepage! I am a genetic epidemiologist working on genome-wide association studies and causal inference models (aka Mendelian Randomization).

Research Objective

The goal of my research is to contribute to personalized medicine via genetic epidemiology. This includes analyzing the molecular sex differences in multiple OMICS levels as well as the different risk profile for various diseases, which are also age-dependent. For this, I use genetic summary statistics from large-scale studies, genetic consortia and electronic health data in causal models of Mendelian Randomization.

Current projects

  • Multivariate MR (MVMR) using longitudinal exposure data
  • Genetics and (MV)MR analyses of steroid hormones
  • Genetics and (MV)MR analyses of PCSK9 levels
  • MVMR of age at menarche and age at menopause on disease risk in women
