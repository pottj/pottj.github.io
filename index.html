Welcome to my homepage! I am a biostatistician working on genetics in causal inference models (aka Mendelian Randomization).

Research Objective

The goal of my research is to better understand the differences between the sexes. This includes both the molecular differences in the different OMICS levels (so far, genomics, transcriptomics, and proteomics) as well as the different and age-dependent risk profile for various diseases, using large-scale studies (e.g. NAKO, UK Biobank, or FinnGen) and electronic health data in causal models of Mendelian Randomization (MR). Taken together, I hope to promote the development of personalized medicine.

Current projects

  • Multivariate MR (MVMR) using longitudinal exposure data
  • MR of steroid hormones on gene expression levels
  • MVMR of age at menarche and age at menopause on disease risk in women

Previous projects

Genome-wide association (meta-analysis) studies (with SNP x sex interactions) of

(sub-)clinical traits

  • Carotid plaques (number of plaques, size of plaques)
  • Pulse wave velocity (PWV)
  • Ankle-brachial index (ABI)
  • Chronic kidney disease (CKD)
  • Smell recognition (Sniffin Sticks)

blood biomarker

  • PCSK9
  • Steroid hormones (including cortisol, aldosterone, progesterone, 17-OHP, DHEA-S, androstenedione, testosterone, and estradiol)
  • Adipokines
  • Phyto- and Zoosterols
  • Olink CVD panel
